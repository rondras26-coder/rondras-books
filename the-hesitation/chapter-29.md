# CHAPTER TWENTY-NINE

## The Reckoning

---

Six months later.

Sarah sat in the courtroom, watching Marcus Webb being led away in chains. The trial had lasted three weeks. The verdict took the jury four hours.

Guilty on all counts.

But Sarah wasn't thinking about Marcus. She was thinking about James.

James Morrison, who had been found in a cabin in Montana, half-starved, delirious. Who had spent four months in a psychiatric facility. Who had refused to see her, refused to speak, refused to acknowledge that any of it had happened.

He was free now. Not healed, not redeemed. Just free.

"Sarah."

She turned. David stood behind her, Lily at his side.

"Let's go," David said. "It's over."

"Is it?" Sarah looked back at the empty courtroom. "Is it really?"
